Mentor a Code for Progress student

Participants are each paired with a mentor who can help them navigate the professional workplace, expand their network, and lead a sustainable life in the social justice movement. Ideal mentors don’t need coding experience, will go through a short orientation with Code for Progress staff, and are available to participants during and after the training to provide support, guidance and encouragement for about an hour a week.

Code for Progress mentor profile

Mentors come in different shapes and sizes – and so do mentees. We expect that we’ll have a range of personalities and skills on our mentoring team, and hope to hear from anyone interested who:
  • Is willing to be available for at least one hour per week for 6 months after the participant residency program
  • Works in the social justice movement
  • Has experience or identifies with a historically excluded community
  • Understands the challenges associated with moving into an unfamiliar work environment or starting a new career
  • Sees the value in developing technology for the social justice movement
  • Can model and advise on maintaining a sustainable work life balance
  • Is willing to use personal networks to help a mentee make connections and develop relationships
  • Balances constructive criticism with encouragement
